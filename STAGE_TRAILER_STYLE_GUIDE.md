# STAGE Trailer Style Guide
## Analysis of 10 Reference Trailers

**Date:** 2026-02-03
**Source:** ~/Desktop/Trailers/ (10 STAGE originals)

---

## 1. DURATION & PACING

### Duration Range
- **Minimum:** 110 seconds (HR_Chalava, HR_Gawahi)
- **Maximum:** 160 seconds (Dada Lakhmi)
- **Average:** ~135 seconds (2 minutes 15 seconds)
- **Standard:** 120-150 seconds

**Key Insight:** STAGE trailers are MUCH LONGER than typical trailers (which are 60-90s). This allows for:
- More story development
- Character establishment
- Multiple dialogue moments
- Detailed narrative setup

### Editing Pace
- **Average cut frequency:** One scene change every 3-4 seconds
- **Fast sections:** Cuts every 1-2 seconds (action sequences)
- **Slow sections:** Holds for 5-8 seconds (dramatic moments, dialogue)
- **Example (Chalava 110s):** 29 scene changes = 3.8s average per shot

### Rhythm Pattern
```
Opening: Slow build (5-8s shots)
    ↓
Early Middle: Faster cuts (2-3s shots)
    ↓
Action Peak: Very fast cuts (1-2s shots)
    ↓
Dialogue Break: Slow hold (5-8s)
    ↓
Build to Climax: Fast cuts again
    ↓
End Card: Slow (3-5s)
```

---

## 2. BRANDING ELEMENTS

### STAGE Logo
- **Placement:** Top-right corner (ALWAYS)
- **Design:** Red/pink split circle icon + "STAGE" text in white
- **Persistence:** Present in 95% of shots (removed only for pure black title cards)
- **Size:** Small, non-intrusive (about 100-120px wide)

### Opening Title
- **Text:** "STAGE PRESENTS" (English, all caps)
- **Timing:** Within first 5-10 seconds
- **Presentation:** Overlaid on action scene OR title card
- **Typography:** Bold, sans-serif, white text
- **Duration:** 2-4 seconds

### Closing Elements
- **Release date/platform info:** Often included
- **Social media handles:** Sometimes present
- **"Available Now" / "Coming Soon":** Common end cards

---

## 3. VISUAL STYLE

### Aspect Ratio
- **Cinematic:** 2.35:1 with black bars (letterbox) - PREFERRED
- **Standard:** 16:9 (full frame) - also common
- **Never:** Vertical or square format

### Color Grading
- **Overall tone:** Desaturated, gritty, cinematic
- **Color palette:**
  - Reduced saturation (70-80% of original)
  - Teal-orange color grading common
  - Darker shadows, crushed blacks
  - Earthy tones for rural settings
- **Mood:** Serious, intense, dramatic

### Resolution
- **YouTube/Premium:** 1080p (1920x1080) or 4K (3840x2160)
- **Meta/Facebook:** 1080p
- **Twitter:** Lower quality for file size

### Shot Types (by frequency)
1. **Medium shots:** Character faces/torsos (40%)
2. **Close-ups:** Intense emotional moments (25%)
3. **Wide shots:** Establishing location/action (20%)
4. **Action shots:** Fights, chases, violence (15%)

---

## 4. TEXT OVERLAYS

### Hindi/Haryanvi Dialogue Cards
- **Frequency:** 6-10 cards per trailer
- **Background:** Pure black OR very dark scene
- **Font:** Bold Devanagari script
- **Color:** White or light blue
- **Size:** Large, centered
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds each
- **Purpose:** Key dialogue lines that tease conflict/drama

**Examples from Chalava:**
- "इस चक्रव्यूह मह सब फसेंगे" (Everyone will be trapped in this trap)

### Characteristics
- Short, punchy lines (5-10 words)
- Often questions or declarations
- Build tension/intrigue
- Character-driven
- Colloquial/regional language

### Title Cards
- Show/movie title
- Actor names (sometimes)
- Director credit (sometimes)
- Platform/release info

## 5. CONTENT THEMES

### Genres (from 10 trailers analyzed)
1. **Crime/Thriller** - Most common
2. **Action/Drama** - Very common
3. **Emotional/Family Drama** - Common
4. **Mystery** - Common
5. **Romance** - Present but secondary

### Common Elements
- **Violence/Action:** Fights, guns, physical confrontations
- **Police/Authority:** Officers, investigations, justice system
- **Rural/Semi-urban settings:** Villages, small towns, local culture
- **Family conflicts:** Revenge, honor, relationships
- **Regional identity:** Haryanvi culture and language prominent

### Character Archetypes
1. **Protagonist:** Strong, determined, often conflicted
2. **Antagonist:** Powerful, threatening, obstacle
3. **Love interest:** Emotional anchor (in romantic subplots)
4. **Authority figure:** Police, elder, judge
5. **Supporting cast:** Friends, family, community

## 6. NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### Act Breakdown (for 120-150s trailer)

#### Act 1: Setup (0-30s)
- STAGE PRESENTS card
- Establishing shots of location
- Introduce protagonist
- Normal world/status quo
- First hint of conflict

#### Act 2: Conflict (30-60s)
- Problem escalates
- Introduce antagonist/threat
- Show stakes
- Dialogue card: Question or challenge
- Action sequence begins

#### Act 3: Escalation (60-90s)
- Multiple fast cuts
- Action montage
- Dialogue cards showing tension
- Relationships tested
- Violence/confrontation

#### Act 4: Peak Tension (90-120s)
- Climactic action
- Emotional peak
- Fastest cuts
- Loud music/sound effects
- Everything at stake

#### Act 5: Resolution Tease (120-150s)
- Slow down pace
- Final dialogue card (powerful line)
- Title card
- Release info
- STAGE branding

## 7. AUDIO STRUCTURE

### Music Style
- **Genre:** Epic orchestral, regional folk fusion, electronic hybrid
- **Build:** Starts soft, builds to loud
- **Drops:** Strategic silence before big moments
- **Percussion:** Heavy use in action sequences
- **Cultural elements:** Haryanvi instruments, folk melodies

### Sound Design
- **Dialogue:** Mostly subtitled, but some voiceover
- **Effects:** Emphasized (gunshots, punches, doors, vehicles)
- **Ambience:** Minimal, music-driven
- **Mix:** Music-forward with strategic silence

### Volume Dynamics
```
0-20s:    Soft entry (30-50% volume)
20-40s:   Building (50-70%)
40-80s:   Full power (90-100%)
80-90s:   Drop/silence (0-20%)
90-120s:  Maximum intensity (100%+)
120-end:  Fade out (80-40%)
```

## 8. PLATFORM VARIATIONS

### YouTube (YT) - Primary
- **Duration:** Longer (120-160s)
- **Quality:** Highest (1080p/4K)
- **Aspect:** Cinematic preferred
- **File size:** No strict limit

### Meta (Instagram/Facebook)
- **Duration:** Slightly shorter (110-140s)
- **Quality:** High (1080p)
- **Aspect:** 16:9 or 2.35:1
- **File size:** Optimized (400-600MB)

### Twitter
- **Duration:** Shortest (110-130s)
- **Quality:** Lower (720p or compressed 1080p)
- **Aspect:** Usually 16:9
- **File size:** Heavily compressed (200-300MB)

## 9. KEY DIFFERENTIATORS

### What makes STAGE trailers unique:

1. **Length:** 2x longer than standard trailers
2. **Regional focus:** Haryanvi/Hindi language and culture
3. **Persistent branding:** Logo always visible
4. **Dialogue-driven:** Text overlays as narrative device
5. **Action-heavy:** Fights and violence prominently featured

## 11. DIALOGUE TEXT CARD GUIDELINES

### Format
- **Language:** Hindi/Haryanvi (Devanagari script)
- **Length:** 6-12 words maximum
- **Line breaks:** Usually 1-2 lines
- **Alignment:** Center
- **Font size:** Very large (readable even on mobile)

### Content Types
1. **Threatening:** "तुम्हारी औकात क्या है?" (Who do you think you are?)
2. **Questioning:** "क्या तुम इसे कर सकते हो?" (Can you do this?)
3. **Declaring:** "अब बदला लूंगा" (Now I'll take revenge)
4. **Emotional:** "मैं तुम्हें छोड़ नहीं सकता" (I can't leave you)
5. **Plot:** "इस गांव में कुछ होने वाला है" (Something is about to happen in this village)

### Timing
- Appears: On beat with music or after dramatic moment
- Duration: Long enough to read 2x (2-3 seconds)
- Frequency: Every 15-20 seconds in dialogue-heavy trailers

## 12. TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### Export Settings (Recommended)

#### YouTube Version
- **Resolution:** 1920x1080 (or 3840x2160 for 4K)
- **Framerate:** 24fps or 25fps
- **Bitrate:** 10-20 Mbps (1080p), 40-50 Mbps (4K)
- **Codec:** H.264 (High Profile)
- **Audio:** AAC, 320kbps, 48kHz

#### Meta Version
- **Resolution:** 1920x1080
- **Framerate:** 24fps or 25fps
- **Bitrate:** 8-12 Mbps
- **Codec:** H.264
- **Audio:** AAC, 192kbps, 48kHz

#### Twitter Version
- **Resolution:** 1280x720 (or compressed 1920x1080)
- **Framerate:** 24fps
- **Bitrate:** 5-8 Mbps
- **Codec:** H.264
- **Audio:** AAC, 128kbps, 44.1kHz

## 14. WHAT TO AVOID

### Don't Include:
1. ❌ Short 60-90s duration (too brief for STAGE style)
2. ❌ Bright, saturated colors (not gritty enough)
3. ❌ English dialogue cards (unless bilingual show)
4. ❌ Vertical or square format
5. ❌ Glossy Bollywood-style shots
6. ❌ Missing STAGE branding
7. ❌ Too much silence (music-driven format)
8. ❌ Comedy/light tone (STAGE = serious/intense)
9. ❌ Urban metro settings only (regional focus important)
10. ❌ Overly complex plot (keep story clear)
